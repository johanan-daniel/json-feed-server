📝 Fact Check by Sabrina Cruz

What Flavour is Banana Flavour?

You may have heard that artificial banana flavour is based on the now-extinct Gros Michel banana variant and that’s why it doesn’t taste like the usual Cavendish banana variant that dominates grocery stores.

Unfortunately, there are 4 problems with this rumour:

  1. Artificial banana flavour is primarily isoamyl acetate, a volatile that basically everyone agrees gives all bananas their banana-y flavour.

  2. The Gros Michel was sweeter and simpler than the Cavendish, which meant it was coincidentally similar to artificial banana flavour.

  3. The Gros Michel isn’t extinct, it just can’t be mass produced to the commercial scale we see in grocery stores because of its vulnerability to Panama disease.

  4. We actually have way more banana variety than you probably think. While the Cavendish is popular is a lot of northern nations, South America and Asia actually have a wide away of bananas to choose from!

So, artificial banana flavour happens to taste similar to the Gros Michel which is not extinct and is just one of the many banana varieties that exist around the world!
